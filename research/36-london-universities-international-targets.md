## PART 2: UNIVERSITIES

### 2A. UK UNIVERSITIES

#### UCL Bartlett School of Planning (Arthur's alma mater)
- **Department**: Bartlett School of Planning, Faculty of the Built Environment
- **Key researchers**:
  - **Dr Lucy Natarajan** — Associate Professor of Urban Planning. Promotes wider engagement in urban decision-making. Co-authored "Engaged Urban Pedagogy: Participatory Practices in Planning and Place-Making" (2026). Research on planning issues where public involvement is undervalued.
  - **Prof Michael Short** — Co-author on participatory urban pedagogy
  - **Dr Muzna Al Masri** — Anthropologist specialising in participatory democracy, including Citizen Assemblies
- **Why Frank**: UCL researchers are actively studying participatory planning tools. Frank could be a research instrument for studying civic listening at scale. Arthur's alumni connection makes this the easiest first approach.
- **Contact**: Via UCL Bartlett website; Lucy Natarajan likely reachable through UCL directory
- **Also relevant**: Council on Urban Initiatives at UCL; Youth Inclusive Urban Governance (YUP) project co-producing digital platforms

#### LSE Cities
- **Department**: LSE Cities, within the London School of Economics
- **Key researchers**:
  - **Prof Ricky Burdett** — Director of LSE Cities and the Urban Age programme. Brings together national/city leaders, academics, designers and civic actors.
  - **Dr Philipp Rode** — Executive Director of LSE Cities. Research on urban governance.
- **Research focus**: Urban Governance research unit; New Urban Governance project (with UN Habitat and UCLG)
- **Why Frank**: LSE Cities investigates how cities can be governed more effectively with social inclusion. Frank provides empirical data on what citizens actually talk about — a direct input to their governance research.
- **Contact**: lse.ac.uk/cities

#### University of Manchester
- **Department**: Manchester Urban Institute; School of Environment, Education and Development
- **Key researchers**:
  - **Prof Liz Richardson** — Professor of Public Administration. Research on participatory urban governance, local politics, public services. Named in LGIU Top 25 Thinkers. **Top UK academic target.**
  - **Prof Diana Mitlin** — Scaling up participatory planning in Africa
  - **Dr Iain Deas** — Reader, urban governance and policy
  - **Prof Seth Schindler** — Professor of Urban Politics and Development
- **Why Frank**: Liz Richardson's entire research agenda is participatory urban governance. Frank as a tool for passive civic listening would be directly relevant to her work.
- **Contact**: humanities.manchester.ac.uk/planning

#### University of Sheffield
- **Department**: Faculty of Social Sciences; Department of Politics and International Relations
- **Relevant work**: National Civic Impact Accelerator; civic university movement
- **Why Frank**: Sheffield positions itself as a civic university working with local authorities and combined authorities. Frank could be a practical tool for their civic engagement commitments.
- **Contact**: sheffield.ac.uk/social-sciences

#### University of Birmingham — INLOGOV
- **Department**: Institute of Local Government Studies (INLOGOV), School of Government
- **Key researchers**:
  - **Dr Sonia Bussu** — Associate Professor. Work on citizens' assemblies, blending top-down and bottom-up participation approaches
  - Published "Rewiring Local Government for Citizen Engagement" — evidence-based roadmap for revitalising local democracy
- **Why Frank**: INLOGOV is THE leading UK academic centre for local government research. Their recent work on "rewiring" local government for citizen engagement is exactly Frank's value proposition. A research partnership could validate Frank academically.
- **Contact**: birmingham.ac.uk/schools/government/departments/local-government-studies

### 2B. US UNIVERSITIES

#### MIT — Civic Media / Senseable City Lab
- **Status**: The Center for Civic Media closed in 2020. However:
  - **Prof Karrie Karahalios** — Full Professor, known for algorithmic audits, social computing, civic technology
  - **Prof Deb Roy** — Research on anonymization of voices in civic dialogue spaces
  - **MIT Senseable City Lab** (directed by Carlo Ratti — CRA connection!) — uses AI and sensor data for urban understanding
- **Why Frank**: The Senseable City Lab connection through CRA is direct. Frank's NLP analysis of civic discourse could feed into their urban sensing research.
- **Contact**: media.mit.edu; senseable.mit.edu

#### Harvard Kennedy School
- **Key researchers**:
  - **Prof Archon Fung** — Winthrop Laflin McCormack Professor of Citizenship and Self-Government. Co-founded Participedia.net (wiki for participatory political processes). Examines how digital tech affects citizens' engagement with government. **Top US academic target.**
  - **Prof Danielle Allen** — Allen Lab for Democracy Renovation. Advancing frameworks for tech and democracy.
  - **Prof Marshall Ganz** — Practicing Democracy Project at Center for Public Leadership
- **Why Frank**: Archon Fung's Participedia project is directly comparable — he catalogues participatory processes worldwide. Frank could be both a subject of study and a tool for his research. Danielle Allen's Democracy Renovation lab focuses on "renovating governance mechanisms."
- **Contact**: hks.harvard.edu; Fung is reachable through the Ash Center

#### NYU GovLab (now at Northeastern)
- **Department**: The Governance Lab, now primarily based at Northeastern University's Burnes Family Center for Social Change
- **Key researcher**:
  - **Prof Beth Simone Noveck** — Co-Founder and Director. Former White House Deputy CTO and Director of Open Government Initiative. Former NJ Chief Innovation Officer.
- **Initiatives**: CrowdLaw (public engagement in lawmaking), Civic Challenges methodology, smarter crowdsourcing
- **Why Frank**: GovLab's CrowdLaw initiative examines how citizens can participate in lawmaking. Frank provides the "listening" layer that complements their "participation" focus. Noveck has the policy connections to champion new civic tools.
- **Contact**: thegovlab.org

#### Stanford — Deliberative Democracy Lab + d.school
- **Deliberative Democracy Lab**:
  - **Prof James Fishkin** — Director. Invented Deliberative Polling, conducted 150+ experiments in 50+ countries.
  - **Dr Alice Siu** — Associate Director. Worked with policymakers in 50+ countries on implementing deliberative democracy.
- **d.school**:
  - **Thomas Both, Nadia Roumani, David Janka** — Multi-year project on design in election administration
  - Civic Design course offered
- **Why Frank**: Fishkin's Deliberative Polling requires understanding what people care about before deliberation. Frank's passive listening could identify the right topics for deliberative processes.
- **Contact**: deliberation.stanford.edu; dschool.stanford.edu
