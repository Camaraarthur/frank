# Video Speaking Points (2 minutes)

Open your phone showing frank.community/beckton. Screen record + talk.

---

## 0:00-0:15 — The problem (hook)

"Democracy has a listening problem. Councils run consultations that get 3% response rates. Surveys measure opinions that don't exist until the question creates them. The people with the most urgent things to say never show up to the meeting. Frank fixes this."

## 0:15-0:40 — What Frank does (show the app)

"Type any area. Frank tells you who governs it, what the data says, what's contested. Then it teaches you how to interview — not to confirm what you think, but to discover what people actually experience."

[Show the issues tab]

"These are real issues from real interviews I conducted in Beckton, East London. Loss of community identity. Property price displacement. A closed ski slope that was the only community landmark. Isolation. Frank scored each one across severity, frequency, cost, complexity."

## 0:40-1:10 — The public page (the product)

[Show the policy tab]

"Each issue generates policy proposals grounded in what the local council can actually do. With costs, drawbacks, who to contact, and evidence from around the world of what worked. This becomes a public page: frank.community/beckton. Anyone can see it. Anyone can add their voice."

[Show voices tab briefly]

"These are the people I spoke to. Their words, anonymised, published with consent. This is what civic intelligence looks like when it starts from the street, not from a spreadsheet."

## 1:10-1:40 — Why it matters

"The thesis is simple. If communities can see what they collectively need, and politicians can see it too, then ignoring it becomes a choice. Frank doesn't lobby. It makes reality visible. What people do with that visibility is democracy."

"Everything is open source. The prompts are auditable. The methodology is published. No one owns this data — it belongs to the community that generated it."

## 1:40-2:00 — The ask

"We have a working prototype, real field data, and a clear roadmap. What we need from Mozilla is 12 months to deploy this in 3-5 London boroughs with real volunteers, real councils, and real residents. We want to prove that structured listening works at scale."

"I'm Arthur. This is Frank. Your community, frank about what it needs."

## Tips:
- Talk to the camera like you're explaining to Mikhail, not presenting to a board
- Show the actual app on your phone, tap through the tabs
- Don't rehearse it perfectly — authentic > polished
